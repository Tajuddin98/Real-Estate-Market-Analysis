**Key Project Highlights**
**Objective**
To analyze real estate market trends across multiple U.S. states using property, pricing, and market performance data.

Data Coverage
Includes data for 5 major states (CA, FL, IL, NY, TX), with breakdowns by property type, city, price, area, and market status.

KPIs Used
Average Bedrooms: 3
Average Property Price: ~$810K
Total Market Value: ~$2.43 Billion
Average Days on Market: 61 days
Total Properties Analyzed: 3000
Total Bedrooms Count: 10.41K

**Property Status Insights**
Majority of listings are Sold or Pending, indicating high market demand.
Fewer properties remain For Sale, showing a fast-moving market.
State-Level Insights
California (CA) has the highest number of properties and highest total market value (~0.53bn).
Texas (TX) shows the fastest property sales (lowest Avg. Days on Market).
Price trend increases as: TX < NY < FL < IL < CA, showing location-driven pricing differences.

**City-Level Observations**
Cities like Los Angeles, Sacramento, San Francisco have higher average area (sqft), indicating premium market zones.
CA cities have the highest overall property counts.

**Visualization Techniques**
Slicers for property type & state for dynamic filtering.
KPI cards for quick metrics.
Bar charts for price & property distribution.
Line chart for area trends across cities.

**Overall Market Conclusion**
The real estate market is active and competitive, with strong demand and higher values concentrated in CA.
Market behavior varies significantly by state, especially in pricing and speed of sales.
